Why Fresh Graduates Need the Right Template

As a new professional, your resume must:

  • Highlight education & projects: Prioritize academic achievements and key coursework.
  • Be ATS‑compatible: Avoid tables, images, or columns that parsing systems can’t read.
  • Show personality subtly: A splash of color or modern typography can make your resume memorable.
  • Be easy to customize: Swap details quickly for different job roles.

How to Access & Customize Templates

  1. Open Google Docs → Template Gallery → Resumes.
  2. Select one of the five templates above.
  3. Click “Use this template” to open it.
  4. Customize with your name, contact info, summary, education, projects, and skills.
  5. Add numbers: “Led team of 5,” “Improved process time by 20%.”
  6. Download as PDF or share via link.

Expert Tips for Graduate Resumes

  • Stick to one page: Highlight only your strongest accomplishments.
  • Use keywords: Mirror language from job postings to pass ATS scans.
  • Lead with a summary: A two-sentence opener clarifies your career goals.
  • Include academic projects: Show relevance with key coursework or capstones.
  • Test with an ATS checker: Catch hidden layout issues before submitting.
  • Keep fonts standard: Stick with Arial, Calibri, or Georgia for compatibility.

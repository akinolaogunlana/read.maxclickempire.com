How the Template Is Structured

This clean, single-page layout uses clear sections for:

  • Header: Name, job title (“Cold Storage Specialist – Remote”), and contact info
  • Professional Summary: 3–4‑line snapshot highlighting remote readiness and freezer expertise
  • Key Skills: Split into Freezer-Specific (e.g., temperature control, pallet jack) and Remote (e.g., virtual collaboration, self-management)
  • Experience: Reverse-chronological with job title, company, remote details, and 3–5 action-oriented bullets
  • Certifications/Education: OSHA, cold-chain training, remote-work tools (Zoom, Slack)

Step-by-Step: Customize & Optimize Your Resume

1. Header & Professional Summary

Set your tone right away:

  • Header format: Name | Cold Storage Specialist (Remote)
  • Summary: “Experienced freezer technician with 5+ years managing -20°C to -40°C storage. Proven record in remote inventory coordination, virtual team oversight, and temperature compliance.”

2. Key Skills Section

  • Freezer-specific: Temperature monitoring, pallet jack operation, HACCP compliance

Formatting tip: use two narrow columns to fit both skill sets neatly.

Answering Common Questions

1. Should I state I'm open to remote work?

Yes. Add “(Remote)” next to your job title and mention it in the summary to signal readiness.

2. Can I include a photo?

Avoid photos—ATS can't parse them, and most U.S. companies discourage images.

3. What if my past experience is on-site only?

Emphasize remote tasks: virtual reporting, digital logs, coordinating via email/Slack—every bit counts.

4. How long should this resume be?

Keep it to one page—unless you have 10+ years of freezer management and remote leadership.

5. Are certifications necessary?

Absolutely. Certifications like OSHA and CCS reinforce your freezer expertise and remote work readiness.

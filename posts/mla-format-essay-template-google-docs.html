MLA Format Essay Template for Google Docs: A Complete Student Guide

If you're a student staring down the blinking cursor of a blank Google Doc, wondering how to format your essay in MLA style, you're not alone. MLA (Modern Language Association) formatting is one of the most commonly required citation styles in high school and college humanities classes — but unless you’ve memorized every spacing, margin, and header rule, it can feel like a maze. The good news? Once you understand the MLA structure and how Google Docs can automate parts of it, you’ll never struggle with formatting again.

What Is MLA Format?

MLA format is a standardized style used primarily in the humanities—think literature, philosophy, and the arts. It governs how your paper should be laid out: margins, font, spacing, headers, in-text citations, and the Works Cited page. While it’s not difficult, consistency is crucial. A misplaced period or an incorrectly aligned header can cost you points on a paper. Fortunately, Google Docs makes it relatively easy to maintain this format once you set it up correctly—or use a reliable template.

Step-by-Step: How to Format an MLA Essay in Google Docs

Step 1: Set Up the Page Layout

Before you start typing, adjust your document's layout:

  • Font: Times New Roman, 12pt
  • Line Spacing: Double
  • Margins: 1 inch on all sides
  • Paragraphs: Indent the first line (use Tab key)

To make these changes in Google Docs, go to Format → Line & paragraph spacing → Double, and then set paragraph indentations under Format → Align & Indent → Indentation options.

Step 2: Create a Proper Header

In the top-left corner of the first page, write your:

  1. Full Name
  2. Instructor’s Name
  3. Course Title
  4. Date (in Day Month Year format — e.g., 28 July 2025)

Align this block to the left margin. Then, on the same page, center the essay title below this block — no bold, underline, or italics unless part of a title.

Step 3: Add Page Numbers with Your Last Name

Click Insert → Page Numbers and choose the top-right option. Then double-click the header, type your last name followed by a space in front of the number.

Step 4: Body of the Essay

Begin your essay with the first line indented and all text double-spaced. Avoid extra spacing between paragraphs. Make sure every quotation and in-text citation follows MLA style.

Step 5: Add the Works Cited Page

Start a new page at the end. Center the title “Works Cited,” then list entries alphabetically. Use hanging indents for each citation.

To create a hanging indent in Google Docs, go to Format → Align & Indent → Indentation options, then under “Special,” choose “Hanging.”

Downloadable MLA Template for Google Docs

If setting it up sounds tedious, you can start with a free MLA template directly in Google Docs. Here’s how:

  1. Go to Google Docs.
  2. Click Template Gallery at the top right.
  3. Look for a template titled “Report (MLA)” — click to open.
  4. Make a copy and replace the placeholder text with your content.

This template already includes correct fonts, spacing, headers, and formatting, so all you need to focus on is writing your content.

Why MLA Formatting Matters

Beyond the grade, learning MLA formatting teaches academic professionalism, attention to detail, and proper citation etiquette — skills you'll use long after school. Consistently formatted essays show instructors that you take your work seriously and respect the guidelines of scholarly writing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the correct font and size for MLA in Google Docs?

A: Use Times New Roman, 12pt font. Google Docs defaults to Arial, so you’ll need to change this manually under the toolbar or Format → Text → Font.

Q: How do I insert a hanging indent in Google Docs?

A: Highlight the citation, go to Format → Align & Indent → Indentation Options, and under “Special,” select “Hanging.”

Q: Is there an official MLA template for Google Docs?

A: Yes. Google Docs includes an “MLA Report” under its template gallery. You can also find MLA-approved versions from university websites or the University of Arizona Global Campus Writing Center.

Q: Do I need a title page in MLA format?

A: Usually, MLA papers do not require a separate title page unless specifically requested by your instructor. The necessary information appears at the top-left of the first page.

Q: How should I format in-text citations in MLA?

A: Use the author’s last name and page number in parentheses, like this: (Smith 22). No comma between the name and page number.

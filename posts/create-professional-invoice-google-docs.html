What's Inside a Professional Invoice

  • Header: Your logo, business name, and contact information
  • Invoice details: Number, issue date, due date
  • Client information: Client’s name, company, and contact details
  • Line items: Description, quantity/hours, rate, subtotal
  • Totals: Subtotal, taxes, discounts, total due
  • Payment terms: Due terms, accepted methods, and late fees
  • Notes: Thank-you note or next steps

2. Add Your Branding

  • Insert your logo: Use Insert → Image and resize neatly.
  • Customize fonts and colors: Stick to branded accents in headings.

3. Fill in Invoice & Client Information

  • Set a clear naming convention: e.g., “INV-2025-001”.
  • Add dates—invoice issue and due date.
  • Fill client details, including name, address, and email.

4. Detail Your Services

  • List each service/product with a clear description.
  • Include quantity or hours, rate, and item total.
  • Use simple tables to maintain structure.

5. Calculate Totals & Taxes

  • Manually sum subtotals, taxes, and discounts.
  • Double-check with a calculator or Spreadsheet if needed.

6. Add Payment Terms & Notes

  • Clarify due terms: “Net 15”, “Due on receipt”, etc.
  • List accepted payment methods—bank, PayPal, etc.
  • Add a polite thank-you note or next-step instructions.

7. Save & Export

  • Proofread carefully for typos, figures, and formatting.
  • Download as PDF (File → Download → PDF Document) to lock your layout.
  • Store a copy in organized Drive folders like “Invoices 2025”.

Tips for Streamlining and Getting Paid Faster

  • Stay consistent: Use sequential invoice numbers.
  • Follow up: Send reminders 3 days before due date.
  • Track payments: Use a simple sheet to mark “Paid” or “Pending”.
  • Offer convenience: Include clickable PayPal links or bank info.

Step-by-Step: Customize Your Template

1. Choose & Copy Your Foundation

  • Select a template from the list above.
  • Open it in Google Docs → File → Make a copy.

2. Design Your Cover Page

  • Insert your logo (Insert → Image).
  • Add proposal title, client’s name, date.
  • Use 24–30 pt font for headline, 12–14 pt for subtext.

3. Write a Compelling Executive Summary

  • Summarize client pain points and your solution in 3–4 sentences.
  • Clarify what sets you apart—experience, results, benefits.

4. Define Scope & Deliverables

  • Use bullet lists or tables to define tasks.
  • Include timelines or milestones (e.g., Week 1: Discovery; Week 4: Launch).

5. Present Pricing & Payment Terms

  • Insert a simple cost table—item, rate, total.
  • Mention payment terms (Net 30, milestone-based, etc.).

6. Highlight Credentials & Case Studies

  • Add a brief “About Us” section with certifications or awards.
  • Include case studies or testimonials with 1–2 results per client.

7. Close with Next Steps & Signature

  • Guide client to “Approve by signing below” or “Schedule kickoff call.”
  • Leave space for signature and date.

8. Polish & Export

  • Proofread for grammar and alignment.
  • Ensure consistent formatting and bullet indents.
  • Export as PDF for sharing (File → Download → PDF).

Pro Tips for Higher Conversion Rates

  • Personalize: Mention the client's name and specific goals.
  • Visualize data: Use graphs or charts—insert via Drawing → Chart.
  • Keep it concise: 5–8 page max; cut fluff.
  • Review mobile view: Use “Mobile preview” in Google Docs add-on.
  • Follow up: Send a quick email if no reply within a week.

PDF ensures formatting stays consistent. Use shareable link only for drafts.

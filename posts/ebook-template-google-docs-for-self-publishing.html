eBook Template Google Docs for Self-Publishing

If you're a writer, entrepreneur, or digital creator, crafting an eBook isn't just about typing into a blank document — it's about building a seamless reading experience that feels polished and professional. Google Docs offers a free, cloud-based alternative to expensive software, and when used right, it can become your ultimate self-publishing ally.

Why Use Google Docs for Your eBook?

Google Docs offers a low-barrier, high-flexibility tool for self-publishing. Whether you're launching a lead magnet, creating a digital product, or writing your first book for Amazon Kindle, here’s why Google Docs stands out:

  • Cloud-based collaboration: Edit and co-author with ease, anywhere.
  • Auto-save and revision history: Never lose a draft.
  • Template accessibility: Easily replicate or modify book layouts.
  • Export versatility: Save as PDF, EPUB, or Word — all free.

Elements of a Professional eBook Template

Before diving into Google Docs, understand the structural elements of a polished eBook:

1. Cover Page

Your cover is your first impression. Include the title, subtitle, author name, and optional graphic. Use center alignment and bold typography.

2. Table of Contents

Insert an automated table using Google Docs' "Table of contents" tool. Make sure each chapter uses proper heading tags like Heading 1 for automatic generation.

3. Introduction or Preface

Set expectations. This is where you state the book's purpose and your authority on the subject.

4. Chapters (Body Content)

Each chapter should start on a new page. Use Heading 1 for titles, Heading 2 for subtopics, and format body text in a clean, legible font like Georgia or Times New Roman (12 pt).

5. Call-to-Actions

Encourage readers to subscribe, leave reviews, or explore your courses or website. CTAs should be persuasive but not intrusive.

6. About the Author

Include a brief biography, credentials, and links to your site or social media (anchor text only).

7. Copyright and Legal Notices

Protect your content with a simple copyright notice. Example: “© 2025 by [Author Name]. All rights reserved.”

Creating Your eBook Template in Google Docs

Let’s build your eBook from the ground up inside Google Docs:

Step 1: Set Up Page Formatting

Go to File → Page Setup and select the following:

  • Paper size: Letter or A4
  • Orientation: Portrait
  • Margins: 1 inch on all sides

Step 2: Define Styles

Use the Styles dropdown in the toolbar to create consistent headers:

  • Title: For cover
  • Heading 1: Chapter titles
  • Heading 2/3: Subsections
  • Normal Text: For main body

Step 3: Insert a Table of Contents

After the cover and before your first chapter, insert a TOC from Insert → Table of contents. Choose with or without links, depending on your final format.

Step 4: Add Page Breaks

Use Insert → Break → Page Break to ensure clean formatting between chapters and sections.

Step 5: Include Hyperlinks (No Raw URLs)

Highlight text and use Insert → Link to embed URLs behind clean anchor phrases like Publish on Kindle or Design Your Cover.

Exporting Your eBook

When your eBook is finalized, choose File → Download and select from:

  • PDF: Ideal for email marketing and lead magnets
  • EPUB: For uploading to Apple Books or Kobo
  • Microsoft Word: Required by Amazon KDP

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I publish a Google Docs eBook on Amazon Kindle?

A: Yes. Export your eBook to Microsoft Word (.docx), then upload it to Kindle Direct Publishing. KDP supports .docx for easy formatting conversion.

Q: Does Google Docs support EPUB format?

A: Not directly. But you can convert a Word or PDF file using free tools like Calibre or online EPUB converters.

Q: Is there a free eBook template for Google Docs?

A: Yes. Many creators share templates, including a ready-made one from this Medium guide.

Q: Can I add images or charts in my Google Docs eBook?

A: Absolutely. Use Insert → Image or Insert → Chart to embed visuals. Make sure images are compressed for web-friendly formats.

Q: What’s the ideal eBook length for self-publishers?

A: For lead magnets or instructional guides, 10–30 pages is standard. For Kindle books, 50–200 pages depending on the topic depth and niche.

Step-by-Step: Use & Customize Your Template

1. Copy into Your Drive

  1. Click a template link → open in Google Docs
  2. Go to File → Make a copy to save your own editable version

2. Fill in Basic Meeting Info

  • Date, start/end time, location or virtual link
  • List attendees and note absentees
  • Add meeting purpose or agenda topic list

3. Capture Notes & Decisions

  • Leave space under each topic for key discussion points
  • Note formal decisions like approvals or agreements
  • Summarize outcomes clearly and concisely

4. Record Action Items

Use a table with columns for:

  • Task description
  • Assigned to
  • Due date

5. Wrap Up & Schedule Next Meeting

  • List date/time for next check-in
  • Add closing notes or reminders

6. Polish & Distribute

  • Proofread names, dates, and tasks
  • Use shared link or download as PDF (File → Download → PDF Document)
  • Store in a centralized folder (e.g., “Team Meeting Minutes 2025”)

Tips for Better Meeting Minutes

  • Use Meeting Notes in Docs: Type @Meeting notes for an auto-built format linked to calendar events 5
  • Include clear headers: Keep headings like “Decisions” or “Action Items” consistent
  • Assign tasks during the meeting: Clarifies responsibility and avoids post-meeting confusion
  • Send summary within 24 hours: Share via email or attach to calendar invite
  • Track past minutes: Maintain version control for transparency and follow-ups

FAQ

1. Can I attach minutes directly to a Google Calendar event?

Yes—use the built-in Meeting Notes feature after typing @Meeting notes, then click “Share & attach” 6.

2. How do I capture tasks during a virtual meeting?

Use the action-items table live in Docs, assign responsibilities, and update due dates right away.

3. Should my minutes include discussion transcripts?

No—summarize key points and decisions. Transcripts often clutter and confuse the next steps.

4. Can I customize template fonts and colors?

Absolutely—update styles via the Styles menu for headings and body text to match your brand.

5. What’s the ideal meeting minutes length?

Keep it concise—1–2 pages is ideal. More than that, summaries lose impact and clarity.

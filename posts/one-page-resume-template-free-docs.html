Why Use a One-Page Resume in 2025?

Recruiters spend just 6–8 seconds scanning your resume. If your most impressive accomplishments aren’t instantly visible, you risk being passed over. A one-page resume forces clarity, brevity, and impact—ideal for students, fresh grads, career switchers, and even seasoned pros targeting focused roles.

When a One-Page Resume Works Best

  • You're applying for entry-level roles
  • You’re switching careers and want to highlight transferable skills
  • You’ve had fewer than 10 years of experience
  • The employer specifically requests a concise resume

Tips for Writing a Powerful One-Page Resume

Start with a Clear Goal

Tailor your resume to the job you’re applying for. Avoid generic summaries. Use keywords from the job description naturally in your bullet points and summary.

Quantify Your Results

Numbers pop. Replace “Managed a team” with “Led a team of 5 and improved delivery speed by 30%.” Specific achievements help you stand out.

Cut the Fluff

Remove filler phrases like “hard-working” or “team player” unless backed with evidence. Focus on unique skills and results.

Make It Visually Scannable

Use consistent formatting. Bold your job titles. Align your dates. Don’t go smaller than 10pt font.

Key Components of a Formal Business Letter

  • Your contact info: Name, company (if any), address, phone, email
  • Date line: Right-aligned at the top; use full date format like “April 15, 2025.”
  • Recipient details: Name, title, company, address
  • Salutation: “Dear Mr./Ms. LastName:”
  • Body: Intro (purpose), middle (supporting info), closing (call to action or next steps)
  • Closing signature: “Sincerely,” space for signature, your typed name and title

How to Customize Your Letter

1. Update Contact & Date

  • Replace placeholder with your full name, company, address, phone, and professional email
  • Ensure the date is current and professionally formatted

2. Set Recipient Information

  • Include full name, title, company name, and mailing address
  • Double‑check spelling and current job title

3. Craft a Clear Salutation

Use the proper greeting: “Dear Dr. Patel:” or “Dear Ms. Johnson:”—avoid generic or overly informal salutations.

4. Write a Structured Body

  • Intro: State why you’re writing—request, complaint, proposal, etc.
  • Detail paragraphs: Provide necessary background or details professionally and succinctly.
  • Conclusion: Clearly state next steps or actions required.

5. Add Professional Closing & Signature

  • Insert “Sincerely,” followed by a 3–4 line space for your handwritten signature
  • Type your name and, if applicable, job title below

Formatting Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Font: Use Times New Roman or Arial, 11–12 pt
  • Alignment: Left-align everything except date line
  • Spacing: Single space paragraphs, double space between sections
  • Avoid: Colloquial phrases, emojis, “To whom it may concern” (unless you really have to)

FAQs

1. Can I use Google Docs templates in Word?

Yes—download as .docx after editing. Just double-check spacing, as margins may shift slightly in Word.

2. How long should a formal business letter be?

Ideal length is one page. Two pages max only if necessary—like for detailed proposals or legal matters.

3. Do I need a handwritten signature?

Optional. You can scan and insert your signature or leave space for an electronic or printed signature.

4. What salutation should I use if I don’t know the recipient’s name?

Try “Dear Hiring Manager:” or “Dear [Department] Team:.” Avoid “To whom it may concern:” if possible.

5. Can I add bullet points in the body?

Yes—bullets improve readability. Make sure they’re formatted consistently and professionally.

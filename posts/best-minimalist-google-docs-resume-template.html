Step-by-Step Customization Walkthrough

1. Access the Template

  1. In Google Docs: File → New → From template gallery → choose “Simple” or “Easy”.
  2. For BeamJobs: open their Minimalist template link and click File → Make a copy.

2. Structure & Section Order

  • Name & Contact: Use 18–24 pt font for your name, 10–11 pt for contact info.
  • Professional Summary: 2–3 lines summarizing experience and value.
  • Skills: List 5–8 core skills, aligned with the job you're targeting.
  • Experience: Reverse-chronological with 3–5 bullet points each.
  • Education / Extras: Use additional sections as needed—certifications, projects, languages.

3. Optimize for ATS & Readability

  • Standard fonts: Arial, Helvetica, or Times New Roman.
  • No images or icons: Keep it text-based for better parsing.
  • White space: Use single spacing, 0.5–1 pt spacing before/after headings.
  • Keywords: Mirror terms from the job ad (e.g., “data analysis,” “project lead,” “Python”).

4. Polishing & Export

  • Proofread for typos and consistency.
  • Test by copying entire content into a plain text editor—if it's messy, adjust spacing or remove hidden formatting.
  • Export as PDF (File → Download → PDF) for the cleanest layout.

Insider Tips to Stand Out

  • Quantify impact: Add numbers—“increased revenue by 20%,” “managed 10-person team.”
  • Use action verbs: “Led,” “Designed,” “Improved,” “Negotiated.”
  • Include a personal URL: Portfolio, LinkedIn, GitHub—short and clean.
  • One-page discipline: If you're junior or mid-level, shrink non-essential items to one page.

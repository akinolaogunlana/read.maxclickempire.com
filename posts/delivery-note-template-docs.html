Why You Need a Delivery Note Template

  • Proof of delivery: Confirms exactly what was sent and received.
  • Professional appearance: Branded templates show you're organized.
  • Saves time: Reuse a template instead of recreating details every time.
  • Clarity for all parties: Lists items, quantities, dates, and signatures to avoid disputes.

Essential Elements of a Delivery Note

  • Title: Clearly labeled “Delivery Note” at the top.
  • Your company info: Name, logo, address, contact.
  • Recipient info: Customer or supplier name, address.
  • Delivery details: Date, reference number, order number.
  • Item table: Description, SKU, quantity dispatched, unit, received quantity.
  • Notes or special instructions: Any delivery specifics or exceptions.
  • Sign-off area: Signature, printed name, and date for both sender and receiver.

5. Add Notes & Instructions

6. Insert Sign-off Fields

  • Create a table row for signatures: Sender’s and receiver’s names, signature, and date.

Best Practices for Delivery Notes

  • Use unique delivery note numbers: e.g., DN-20250708-001.
  • Archive digitally: Save PDFs in folders by customer or month.
  • Send quickly: Email PDF right after dispatch.
  • Confirm receipt: Ask recipient to return signed copy or scan.
  • Keep printed backup: Drivers can handle paper copies if needed.

FAQs

1. What’s the difference between a delivery note and an invoice?

A delivery note lists items delivered; an invoice bills for them. Use both to ensure accurate billing and tracking.

2. Can I use a Google Sheets version?

Yes, but Docs templates print better. Sheets are useful if you want to auto-calculate quantities or track totals.

3. Is a signature mandatory?

Not legally, but a signed delivery note provides proof of receipt and avoids later disputes.

4. How should I number delivery notes?

Use a sequential format: YEAR-DN-VENDOR/CLIENT-001 (e.g., 2025-DN-ABC-007).

5. Can I email an editable link?

It’s better to send a PDF. Editable links risk data changes and may confuse recipients.

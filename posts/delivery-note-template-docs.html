What Is a Delivery Note?

A delivery note is a document that accompanies a shipment of goods. Unlike an invoice, it doesn’t show prices or payment terms. Instead, it lists the items included in a delivery, serving as proof that goods were dispatched and received. It’s useful for both sender and recipient to verify what was shipped and what was accepted.

Why It Matters

  • Customer confidence: Recipients see exactly what they received, building transparency.
  • Dispute reduction: Fewer "I didn’t get this item" moments when there’s documentation.
  • Internal tracking: Businesses track deliveries across vendors and drivers.
  • Returns and exchanges: Easier to manage when there’s a clear paper trail.

Key Elements of a Delivery Note

For your delivery note to be effective and professional, it must include specific information. A solid template should have:

  1. Document title: Clearly labeled “Delivery Note” or “Delivery Slip.”
  2. Business details: Your logo, name, address, contact number, and email.
  3. Delivery note number: A unique reference for tracking.
  4. Date of delivery: When the items were shipped or received.
  5. Recipient information: Name, company (if applicable), and shipping address.
  6. List of items: Quantity, description, and any identifying SKU or code.
  7. Signature fields: Space for sender and recipient signatures, or digital confirmation.
  8. Optional notes: Return policies, disclaimers, or delivery instructions.

How to Use a Delivery Note Template in Google Docs

Google Docs gives you an accessible, shareable way to customize and manage delivery notes. Here's how to start:

Step 1: Access the Template

Visit docs.google.com and either select a blank document or import a delivery note template (see links below). Choose one with editable fields to save time later.

Step 2: Add Your Company Branding

Insert your business logo at the top of the page. Update the sender section with your business name, address, phone, and email. Keep font styles consistent for a clean, professional look.

Step 3: Set Up the Delivery Details

Add a unique delivery note number and input the date. Below that, fill in the customer’s name and delivery address. You can create a dropdown or a table for quick selection if you serve repeat clients.

Step 4: List the Items

Use a table format to list each item. Include the following columns:

  • Item name or description
  • SKU or item number
  • Quantity
  • Condition (optional)

Keep the formatting simple and aligned so it’s easy to read, even when printed or viewed on mobile.

Step 5: Include Signature Lines

At the bottom of the note, add a line for “Delivered by” and “Received by.” If you don’t do in-person deliveries, use digital acknowledgment by adding a checkbox or editable initials field.

Step 6: Save and Send

When the note is complete, go to File > Download > PDF to export the file. You can attach it to your shipment email or include it in the physical delivery package. If your team works remotely, share it directly through Google Drive with view-only permissions.

Best Practices for Delivery Notes

Consistency and clarity are key. Here are some best practices when using delivery note templates in Google Docs:

  • Version control: Always duplicate the master template before editing. This prevents overwriting your base format.
  • Keep backups: Store delivery notes by month or client in Google Drive folders for easy access and compliance.
  • Integrate with orders: Match delivery notes with invoices or purchase orders to streamline logistics and minimize errors.
  • Include a QR code (optional): For larger operations, link to tracking info, delivery confirmation, or reorder pages.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What’s the difference between a delivery note and an invoice?

A: A delivery note lists what was delivered, while an invoice requests payment. The delivery note may not show prices, while invoices always do.

Q: Is a delivery note legally required?

A: In many industries, it’s not legally required but highly recommended. It serves as a best practice for logistics and customer service.

Q: Can I use Google Docs delivery notes for international shipments?

A: Yes, but include additional customs info like HS codes or country of origin if applicable. For full compliance, consult your shipping carrier.

Q: How do I customize a delivery note template for recurring orders?

A: Duplicate the previous delivery note in Google Docs and update only the variable fields like date, items, or quantities.

Q: Can delivery notes be digital only?

A: Absolutely. You can email PDFs or share links via Google Drive. Just make sure your customer confirms receipt digitally or via email reply.

Who Should Use This?

What to Include in an Employee Timesheet Template

To be functional and clear, your timesheet should capture all the essentials needed for payroll or project management. Here’s what to include:

Core Fields to Track

  1. Employee Name
  2. Pay Period or Week Ending Date
  3. Days of the Week (Mon–Sun)
  4. Start Time and End Time
  5. Total Hours Worked
  6. Breaks or Lunch Time (optional)
  7. Overtime Hours
  8. Comments or Notes
  9. Employee Signature or Initial

Additional Fields for Larger Teams

  • Department or Job Code
  • Supervisor Approval Line
  • Hourly Rate or Pay Total (optional for privacy)

Option 2: Build Your Own from Scratch

If you have unique needs, you can easily build your own table in Google Docs. Here’s how:

  1. Open a blank document at docs.google.com
  2. Insert a table (e.g., 8 columns × 10 rows)
  3. Label headers: Date, Day, In, Out, Break, Total Hours, Notes, Signature
  4. Freeze the header row for easier scrolling (in Google Sheets if preferred)
  5. Save the layout and reuse it weekly or monthly

Option 3: Use Google Sheets Instead

While this article focuses on Google Docs, Google Sheets offers auto-calculation features. If math automation is important, use a Sheet version instead. You can embed it into Docs if needed or print directly from Sheets.

How to Use the Timesheet Template Effectively

Daily Use for Employees

  • Log hours as soon as you start and end work
  • Use the “Comments” section to explain irregular hours
  • Sign at the end of each week to confirm accuracy

Weekly Use for Managers

  • Review submissions every Friday or Monday
  • Confirm totals and signatures
  • Approve or request corrections before payroll

Tips for Teams

  • Store templates in a shared Google Drive folder
  • Use version history to track changes
  • Create a master copy that can’t be edited—have team members duplicate it weekly

Printable or Digital? Choose What Fits Your Workflow

One of the biggest advantages of using Google Docs is flexibility. You can print timesheets for pen-and-paper use—or keep them fully digital. Here’s when to choose each:

Use Printable Timesheets If:

  • Your team prefers handwriting entries
  • Internet access is limited at work sites
  • You need physical records for audits

Use Digital Timesheets If:

  • You want real-time updates and backups
  • You’re managing remote or hybrid teams
  • You want to collaborate or comment on entries

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is there a free employee timesheet template in Google Docs?

A: Yes. You can find templates online or create one yourself in Google Docs. They're free to copy and use, with no restrictions.

Q: Can I calculate total hours automatically in Google Docs?

A: No, Google Docs doesn't calculate totals automatically. For that, switch to Google Sheets, which supports formulas and time functions.

Q: How do I share a timesheet template with my team?

A: Use the "Share" button in the top-right of Google Docs. Set permissions (view or edit), or send a copy via “File > Make a copy.”

Q: Can I customize the template for biweekly or monthly use?

A: Absolutely. Just extend the table rows to match your pay period and adjust the headers. You can even label sections for Week 1 and Week 2.

Q: Do I need a Google account to use the template?

A: Yes, for editing or copying in Drive. However, anyone can view or print it if you share it publicly or as a PDF.

Critical Clauses Every Sales Contract Needs

  • Parties: Your company and your buyer’s name, address, and contact info.
  • Scope of Goods/Services: Detailed description of what’s being sold.
  • Price & Payment Terms: Total amount, currency, due dates, and accepted payment methods.
  • Delivery & Risk Transfer: How, when, and where goods are delivered; when liability shifts.
  • Term & Termination: Contract start date, duration, and grounds for early termination.
  • Warranties & Liabilities: Product quality guarantees, limit on damages.
  • Confidentiality: Protection of proprietary info shared during contract.
  • Dispute Resolution: Method (mediation, arbitration), governing law, and jurisdiction.
  • Signatures: Spaces for electronic or handwritten signatures and dates.

Step‑by‑Step: Customize Your Sales Contract

1. Copy it to Your Drive

Open the template → go to File → Make a copy. Rename it clearly: e.g., “SalesContract_YourCompany_ClientName_Date”.

2. Add Your Company Branding

  • Insert your logo at top left/right via Insert → Image.
  • Update your business name, address, email, and phone.

3. Define the Parties

  • Replace placeholder info with correct legal names and addresses.
  • Ensure accuracy to prevent contract void issues.

4. Clarify Scope of Sale

  • Describe goods or services in detail—model numbers, specs, quantities.
  • Include itemization or reference Appendix if needed.

5. Specify Payment & Price

  • Enter delivery price or rate, tax treatment and currency.
  • Define payment schedules—e.g., 50% deposit, remainder on delivery.
  • List acceptable payment methods.

6. Set Delivery Terms & Risk Transfer

  • Pick Incoterm if shipping goods (e.g., FOB, DDP).
  • Include estimated delivery date/timeframe.

7. Insert Warranties & Liability Limitations

  • Declare your warranty—30 days from delivery, or as needed.
  • Limit liability—for instance, total contract value only.

8. Add Legal Boilerplate

  • Force Majeure: Circumstances beyond control like natural disasters.
  • Governing Law: e.g., “This Agreement is governed by the laws of [State/Country].”
  • Dispute Resolution: Stipulate mediation/arbitration before courts.

9. Finalize and Format

  • Check headings for consistency—use Docs’ Styles.
  • Insert signature lines: Party name, signature, printed name, date.
  • Use numbered paragraphs for legal clarity.

Professional Tips for Sales Contracts

  • Always review basics: Verify spelling, dates, amounts before sending.
  • Highlight changes: Use Comments or track changes when negotiating.
  • Use version control: Name drafts clearly (e.g., Client_V1, V2).
  • Seek legal input: For complex deals or large amounts.

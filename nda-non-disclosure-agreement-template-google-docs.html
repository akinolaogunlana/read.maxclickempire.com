Google Docs NDA (Non‑Disclosure Agreement) Template

Need a clean, editable Non‑Disclosure Agreement template in Google Docs that you can customize, sign, and send within minutes? This guide delivers practical options for both one‑way and mutual NDAs built for business confidentiality.

Why Use a Google Docs NDA Template?

Using Google Docs for your NDA provides:

  • Instant access and editing: Customize names, dates, definitions, and clauses directly in Docs.
  • Easy collaboration: Share the draft with legal counsel or stakeholders for real‑time input.
  • Formal layout: Keeps headings, signature lines, and margins intact for printing or PDF export.
  • Template flexibility: Switch between one‑way (disclosing party only) or mutual versions without reformatting.

Types of NDA Templates Available

Use when only one party shares confidential information, such as during pitch meetings or employee hiring. Cooley GO offers a one‑way NDA designed for one‑sided disclosures.

Mutual NDA

Ideal when both parties exchange proprietary information—such as during partnerships, vendor talks, or collaboration. Template.net and TheGoodocs both offer mutual NDA formats in Docs.

Essential Clauses to Include

A complete NDA template usually includes:

  • Definitions of confidential information and exclusions
  • Disclosing and receiving party identification
  • Purpose and permitted use of the shared information
  • Duration of confidentiality obligations (often survival term post‑termination)
  • Mutual or unilateral obligations
  • Exceptions (public domain, independently developed, third‑party sources)
  • Remedies for breach (injunctive relief, indemnity)
  • Return or destruction of confidential information
  • Governing law and jurisdiction
  • Signature lines and dates

LegalTemplates’ example NDA provides many of these clauses with clear structure and updates as of May 2025.

How to Use a NDA Template in Google Docs

  1. Open the template in Google Docs by choosing “Make a copy” to your Drive.
  2. Select whether the agreement is one‑way or mutual, then customize party details and dates.
  3. Define the scope of confidential information, permitted uses, term, and exclusions.
  4. Review disclosure obligations and customization of termination or survival clauses.
  5. Finalize formatting, adjust spacing if needed, and add page‑by‑page initials or witness lines.
  6. Invite collaborators or legal advisors via share link to review the draft.
  7. Export as PDF and request signatures, or use integrated e‑sign tools if desired.

Best Practices Before Signing

  • Clarify whether information includes IP, financial, or personal data, and confirm exclusions are narrow.
  • Keep duration reasonable—many NDAs expire five years post‑disclosure unless otherwise specified (as in Google’s public agreements).
  • Ensure compliance with local or industry-specific rules—some broad NDAs may conflict with labor or whistleblower protections.
  • Ask every signing party to initial each page and sign with dates in original copies.
  • Store final signed versions as PDFs and retain both digital and physical copies.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I use a free Google Docs NDA template?

A: Yes. Free templates are widely available from sources like TheGoodocs, Template.net, DocsAutomator, and LegalTemplates—fully editable in Google Docs.

Q: What’s the difference between a unilateral and mutual NDA?

A: A unilateral NDA binds only one party not to disclose, suitable for employer‑employee, investor pitches. A mutual NDA binds both parties—used when confidential information is shared both ways.

Q: How long should confidentiality last?

A: Many agreements set survival terms of three to five years after signing or termination. Some templates, like Google's device access NDA, specify five‑year expiry.

Q: Are free templates legally enforceable?

A: Templates provide structure but must be customized to your jurisdiction and situation. Courts may invalidate overly broad terms. Consider legal review if necessary.

Q: Can I automate NDA generation from Google Forms?

A: Yes. Tools like DocsAutomator and solutions based on Google Forms allow placeholder merging—for example, using {{PartyName}} to auto-fill in a Doc template from form responses.

Q: Does NDA need signatures from both parties?

A: If it’s a mutual NDA, both parties must sign. For unilateral NDAs, the receiving party signs and discloses acceptance; signature lines and dates must be included and executed properly.

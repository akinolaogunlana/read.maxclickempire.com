Why Use a Sublease Agreement?

A sublease agreement documents the arrangement between the original tenant (“sublessor”) and the new occupant (“sublessee”). It clarifies responsibilities, rent terms, deposits, duration, and property condition expectations. Crucially, it also ensures compliance with the original lease and landlord’s policies.

What to Include: Section by Section

1. Parties & Premises

Clearly specify both parties (sublessor and sublessee), and the physical address being subleased. If it’s a single room within a larger unit, state that explicitly.

2. Term of Sublease

State the exact start and end dates. Ensure your sublease does not extend beyond the original lease, unless there’s landlord approval.

3. Rent & Utilities

Define the monthly rent amount, due date, and payment method. Also clarify who pays for utilities, Wi‑Fi, or shared services.

4. Security Deposit

Specify the deposit amount, where it's held, conditions for return, and procedures for deductions beyond normal wear and tear.

5. Relationship to Original Lease

Reference the original lease agreement, including date and landlord name. Confirm that the sublessee agrees to abide by all the original lease terms.

6. Condition & Occupants

Document the property condition at move‑in. List permitted occupants. Include a signature clause confirming acceptance of the condition and rules.

7. Termination & Defaults

Include terms for early termination, required notice periods, and consequences of non‑payment or damages.

8. Additional Terms

You can adjust clauses for pets, subtenant visitors, lease extensions, or property modifications. Ensure any changes are signed in writing by both parties.

Tenant Checklist Before Subletting

  • Review your original lease to confirm whether subleasing is allowed.
  • Obtain written landlord consent if required.
  • Vet your subtenant carefully—the original tenant remains responsible under the lease.
  • Ensure all terms are clearly documented—avoid verbal-only agreements.
  • Consult local laws—some regions mandate written sublease terms or landlord disclosure requirements.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I sublease without the landlord's permission?

A: Only if your original lease explicitly allows it. Otherwise, you risk breach of contract or eviction. It's always safest to get written consent.

Q: What happens if the subtenant doesn’t pay rent?

A: As the sublessor, you remain responsible under the original lease. You may lose your deposit or face penalties if subtenant rent defaults occur.

Q: Can the sublease extend past the original lease term?

A: Not without landlord approval. The sublease should end on or before the original lease expiration to prevent conflicts or invalid contract terms.

Q: Is an oral sublease agreement valid?

A: In many jurisdictions, a sublease must be written to be enforceable. Verbal terms are harder to prove and often not legally binding.

Q: How is a sublease different from assignment?

A: A sublease keeps the original tenant on the lease—you're essentially renting part of the space. Assignment transfers the lease completely to another party, with landlord approval.
